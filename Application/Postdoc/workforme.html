Work for Me Seminar

A collaborative graduate mathematics seminar where each session focuses on a single participant's proposed idea. Participants collectively solve and refine mathematical problems as a team.

Structure

  • Proposal Stage: Each week, one participant proposes a mathematical idea or research challenge. The proposal should be concise, with clear motivations and goals.
  • Acceptance Stage: The group discusses the proposed ideas. Once an idea is accepted, it becomes the week's focus.
  • Collaborative Work: Until a proposal is accepted, all members contribute to shaping and refining ideas. After acceptance, the group helps the proposer through active collaboration, feedback, and problem-solving.
  • Follow-Up: At the beginning of the next session, the proposer presents progress and outcomes, followed by a new proposal cycle.

Guidelines

  • Proposals should be concise, clear, and feasible within the seminar framework.
  • All participants must actively contribute and engage in discussions.
  • The seminar emphasizes collaborative learning and creativity over competition.

Expected Outcomes

  • Participants gain diverse perspectives and improve problem-solving skills.
  • Research ideas are refined and advanced through collaborative efforts.
  • A supportive and engaging academic community is fostered.
