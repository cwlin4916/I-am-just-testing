Grading

What would be a reasonable Calculus grading rubric? These are computational, and often, there is a preferred method. There are thus two aspects to such problems

  • Knowing the method.
  • Executing the method. This is also referred as computation below.
I write a list below of the 2x2 possible grades out of 1.
  • 1 :: correct method and computation
  • 0.7 :: correct method but computational mistake,
  • 0.5 :: correct answer but incorrect method/ correct computations and irrelevant method.
  • 0.2 :: incorrect method and calculation - nice attempt!
The reason I thus prefer a negative scheme is that most people are either in the first or second case. There are of course other scenarios where we might want to deduct points. I list a few below
  • -0.1 Repeated mistake.
  • -0.1 if using tricks which are unexplaiend.
  • -0.3 to -0.6 for interpretng question incorrectly.
