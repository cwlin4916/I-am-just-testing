Weekend Projects in Machine Learning

Below is a list of weekend projects I have done during my free time.
Topics range from multilingual NLP, computational social science, and memory networks.

Current Projects:

  • Geometry of parameter spaces, On-going. Joint with Chris Hillar (Redwood Research).
    We study the relation between polytopal decomposition, scaling, and optimization in the parameter space of neural networks.
    Our work is inspired by recent works which apply tropical geometry.
    [code]
  • Scaling and generalization of associative memory networks, On-going. Joint with Muhan Gao (Johns Hopkins University).
    This project empirically studies dense associative memory networks as developed by Krotov and Hopfield in language modeling tasks.
    We focus on scenarios where stored memories exceed theoretical capacity limits.
    [code]

Past projects:

  • Multilingual synthetic dataset generation. Joint with Chutong Meng (George Mason University).
    This project studies synthetic data in spoken language understanding (SLU).
    [code]
  • AnthroScore: A Multilingual NLP Tool for Social Science; April 2024.
    This small note plays around with anthropomorphism, the attribution of human traits to non-human entities, using recent methodologies.
    [code]
  • Adversarial debates for multiple language models. Joint with Cole Molloy (Johns Hopkins University) and Lois Wang (Johns Hopkins University).
    We structured in-context adversarial debates between language models to mitigate biases in pretrained models.
    [code]
  • Embedding space analysis of multilingual models, May 2024.
    Analyzes the spatial organization of point clouds induced from word embeddings.
    It discusses interpretations of isotropy across different languages and models.
    [code]
  • Emotion fine tuning, October 2024. An experimental project with Prof. Levine (Cornell University) and Luhan A. Mikaelson (Apart Research).
    We explored the role of emotional feedback compared to reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) in improving model performance.
